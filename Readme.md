🔍**Overview**

This project analyzes loan portfolio data to uncover insights related to risk, customer behavior and data quality. The dashboard is built using Power BI & SQL and focuses on identifying key trends in loan distribution, defaults and delinquency.

🎯 **Objectives**

Analyze loan distribution across different dimensions (Grade, State, Age, Purpose)
Identify default and delinquency patterns
Highlight data quality issues (missing values)
Provide actionable business recommendations

📁 **Dataset Summary**
Total Loan Amount: ₹732M
Total Loans: 65K
Total Collection: ₹808M

📌 **Key Insights**

Total collections exceed disbursed loan amount, indicating strong revenue generation through interest income and effective recovery mechanisms.

Loan disbursement peaked during 2017–2018 and declined in 2019, potentially due to tighter credit policies or early signs of economic slowdown rather than COVID-related impact.

🔹 Risk Metrics
Default Loans: 1K (1.56%)
Delinquent Loans: 7K (10.84%)
Source verified loans show the highest default (3.11%) and delinquency rates (11.4%), indicating that partial verification may not be sufficient to mitigate risk and could be masking higher-risk borrowers.
Delinquency rates are consistent (~11–12%) across all loan statuses, indicating that temporary payment delays are common but do not necessarily lead to default, as many loans recover and reach full repayment.

💡 **Key Recommendations**
1. Improve Data Quality
Make Grade, Loan status, verification status & State mandatory fields
Implement validation checks in data entry systems
2. Strengthen Risk Monitoring
Build early warning systems for delinquency
Monitor high-risk segments closely
Make full verification mandatory for High loan amounts & Risky segments
To reduce delinquency, implement early warning systems, strengthening automated payment mechanisms, and applying targeted interventions for high-risk segments. Additionally, improving customer engagement through reminders and incentives can significantly enhance repayment behavior.
4. Target High-Value Segments
Focus marketing on 26–35 age group
Customize loan offerings based on purpose
5. Optimize Loan Tenure
Encourage shorter tenure loans to reduce risk exposure
6. Simplify Verification Process
Re-evaluate verification steps to balance risk and user experience
